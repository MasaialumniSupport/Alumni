Financial & Contingency Support

Explore the financial resources available to you as a Masai School alumnus, from emergency aid to loan guidance. We are committed to supporting you through every stage of your career journey.

Eligibility for EMI Support

We are here to support you during difficult times. You are eligible for EMI support if you meet any of the following criteria:

  • Company Shutdown: If your company goes out of business and winds up operations.
  • Company Layoffs: If you have experienced a layoff from your previous employer.
  • Major Pay Cuts: If you have suffered significant pay cuts that have impacted your financial stability.

Steps to Avail Financial Support for EMI Payments

To avail financial support for EMI payments, you must complete the following steps. Please note, this support is contingent on your active participation in the placement process, including clearing mock assessments and practicing with the placement team.

All documents will be verified and any request which comes before the 25th of the month will be processed for EMI support after verification. Anything which is asked after the 25th of the month, support will be moved to the upcoming month for verification.

Please be aware: EMI/Contingency support will only be provided once you have completed the placement support steps within the 7-day window for connecting with the Alumni Support team. The amount will be processed only after your documents are delivered to us.

  1. Fill & Submit the Alumni Contingency Form: To receive the form, please reach out to the alumni support team. The team will evaluate and verify your request, and get back to you within two weeks.
  2. Submit Required Documents: Please send the following documents to the office address mentioned below before the 1st of the month for which you have requested for contingency:
    • Filled and signed copy of the contingency application form
    • Signed copies of the cheques
    • Signed copy of the Letter of Intent

Placement & Career Support

Our structured placement process is designed to help you land your ideal job. The steps below detail the path from preparing your profile to receiving an offer. Completing these steps is crucial for accessing placement support.

Part A: Resume, Portfolio & GitHub (RPG) Clearance

Step 1: Update your RPG. Ensure your Resume, Portfolio, and GitHub are current and reflect your latest skills and projects.

Step 2: Submit the form. Complete the RPG form and upload your updated documents.

Step 3: Review the guidelines. For detailed instructions on RPG clearance, please refer to the RPG Clearance Steps.

Part B: Hukumu Interview & Practice Interviews

Platform: Hukumu Interview Platform

Objective: This is a human-based interview where you need to score a minimum of 5 marks.

Outcome:

  • Score below 5: You'll be added to the Continued Curriculum for further skill enhancement.
  • Score 5 or above: You'll receive placement opportunities and also be part of the Continued Curriculum to brush up your skills.

Practice: To enhance your readiness, we recommend using Placed.tech, a platform for mock interviews and skill-based evaluations.

Part C: Alumni Code of Conduct

Review: Please go through the Masai School Code of Conduct.

Acknowledgment: You will be required to sign and acknowledge this document and submit it as part of the RPG form when you upload your updated documents.

Important Notes

  • Process Duration: The completion of these steps takes a maximum of 7 days, depending on your responsiveness and involvement. Failure to complete them within this timeframe will result in ineligibility for EMI and Placement support.
  • Support Suspension: Delays may lead to the suspension of your EMI and Placement support.
  • Workspace Transition: Upon starting placement support, you'll be added to the Placement workspace on Slack and deactivated from the Alumni workspace. You'll be re-added to the Alumni workspace after you restart your career.
  • Support: For any questions, please email us at alumni@masaischool.com.

RBI Guidelines on Unauthorized Electronic Transactions

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) has guidelines to protect you from unauthorized financial transactions. It's crucial to know your rights and responsibilities to protect yourself.

  • ‚úÖ
    Zero Liability: You have zero liability if the fraud is not due to your negligence and you report it within 3 working days of receiving the transaction alert.
  • ‚ö†Ô∏è
    Limited Liability: Your liability is limited if you report the fraud between 4 and 7 working days. The amount depends on the transaction value and your account type.
  • ‚ùå
    Customer Negligence: If the loss is due to your own negligence (e.g., sharing your OTP, PIN, or password), you are liable for the full amount until you report the fraud. After reporting, the bank is responsible for any further losses.

Remember: The most critical step is to report any unauthorized transaction to your bank immediately.
